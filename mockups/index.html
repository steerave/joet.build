Tonal probes

Three directions for joet.build, each rendered with your real content (name, tagline, stats, one case study). Open each in a new tab and compare.

Option A

Editorial / Op-ed →

Cream paper, serif display (Fraunces), generous whitespace, single-column. Reads like a New Yorker profile or Stripe press piece. Lets your case studies do the work as long-form writing rather than dashboards.

Reference: stripe.com/newsroom, ft.com, every.to

Option B

Boardroom / Consulting deck →

Off-white, navy ink, gold accent. Numbered exhibits, bar-chart stats, capabilities document framing. Reads like a McKinsey or BCG client deck. Heaviest signal of seniority and rigor; least personal.

Reference: mckinsey.com/featured-insights, bcg.com/publications

Option C

Quiet executive / Linear-style →

Closest to your current dark palette but with the developer cosplay removed. No mono labels, no terminal breadcrumb, no numbered card indices, no side stripes. Modern sans (Inter Tight), generous breathing room, one accent, one CTA. CV download lives in the nav.

Reference: linear.app, vercel.com, railway.com
